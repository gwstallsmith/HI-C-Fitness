Upper Body Weightlifting

Upper body weightlifting involves using weighted equipment like dumbbells, barbells, and machines.

Those who use weighted equipment typically want to see their muscle mass and strength increase.

Below are weightlifting exercises for people with gym experience ranging from beginner to intermediate.

Barbell Bench Press

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Chest, Arms, Shoulders

Description: Grip barbell with hands slightly further than shoulder width apart. Push up until arms are straight. Pull weight down to your chest. Push back up.

Click me for a video example!

Dumbbell Curls

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Arms, Biceps, Forearms

Description: Grip dumbbells underhanded. Start with the weight at your hips. Keep shoulders and elbows in place. Pull the weight up. Once at the top, control the weight back down to your hips.

Click me for a video example!

Dumbbell Overhead Press

Level: Intermediate

Muscle groups: Shoulders, Arms

Description: Grip dumbbells overhand, above your shoulders. Push up until arms are straight. Once at the top, control the weight back down to your shoulders.

Dumbbell Bench Press

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Chest, Arms, Shoulders

Description: Grip barbell with hands slightly further than shoulder width apart. Push up until arms are straight. Pull weight down to your chest. Push back up.

Lat Pulldown

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Back, Lats

Description: Sit in seat, put legs under cusion. Reach up, and grip bar about shoulder width apart. Pull down to your chest. Control the weight back up.

Click me for a video example!

Overhead Tricep Extension

Level: Intermediate

Muscle groups: Arms, Triceps

Description: Grip dumbbell underhanded. Start with the weight behind your head. Keep shoulders and elbows in place. Push the weight up. Once at the top, control the weight back down behind your head.

Click me for a video example!

Barbell Row

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Arms, Back, Lats

Description: Grip barbell underhanded. Start with the weight at your hips. Pull weight straight into your chest. Once at the top, control the weight back down behind your hips.

Click me for a video example!

Dumbbell Shrug

Level: Intermediate

Muscle groups: Shoulders, Traps

Description: While standing, grip dumbbells at your sides. Shrug your shoulders up while keeping your arms straight. Once at the top, control the weight back down to your sides.

Click me for a video example!

Safety is of the utmost concern in the gym.

Do not attempt intermediate workouts until you have tried the beginner workouts and feel more confident in the gym.
