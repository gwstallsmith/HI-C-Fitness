Upper Body Calisthenics

Upper body calisthenics involve using your body as the weight for the workouts.

Those who do calisthenics typically want to see their muscles gain tone and increase their endurance.

Calisthenics are great because everybody can do them! You don't need any equipment to do most of these exercises!

Below are calisthenic exercises for people with gym experience ranging from beginner to intermediate.

Push-up

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Chest, Arms

Description: Start with your body straight, and your chest touching the ground Place hands directly under your shoulders. Keep legs and back straight and push against the ground. Once at the top, control your body back to the ground.

Click me for a video example!

Dips

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Arms

Description: Start under a bar, or something else to hold onto to. Spread your arms slightly further than shoulder width apart. Grip the bar overhanded. Pull your body up. Once at the top, control your body back down.

Click me for a video example!

Chin-up

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Arms, Back

Description: Start under a bar, or something else to hold onto to. Spread your arms slightly further than shoulder width apart. Grip the bar overhanded. Pull your body up. Once at the top, control your body back down.

Click me for a video example!

Pull-up

Level: Intermediate

Muscle groups: Arms, Back

Description: Start under a bar, or something else to hold onto to. Spread your arms slightly further than shoulder width apart. Grip the bar underhanded. Pull your body up. Once at the top, control your body back down.

Click me for a video example!

Burpee

Level: Intermediate

Muscle groups: Everything

Description: Start crouched. Kick your legs back and fall to the ground with your arms catching you. Kick your legs up to your hands and back under your. Jump and reach into the air. Reach up and jump.

Click me for a video example!

Face Pull

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Arms, Shoulders

Description: Start under a bar placed low to the ground. Spread your arms slightly further than shoulder width apart. Grip the bar overhanded. Kick feet out and put weight on your heels. Pull your body up. Once at the top, control your body back down.

Click me for a video example!

Band Pull Apart

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Shoulders, Back

Description: Start standing. Grip band. Pull band apart until you are making a "T" pose. Control the weight back together.

Click me for a video example!

Band Upright Row

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Arms, Shoulders

Description: Tuck band under your feet. Put both hands together and grab opposite end of band. Pull up to your chin. Control the weight back down.

Click me for a video example!

Safety is of the utmost concern in the gym.

Do not attempt intermediate workouts until you have tried the beginner workouts and feel more confident in the gym.
