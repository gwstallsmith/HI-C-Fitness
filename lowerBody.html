Lower Body Workouts
by Semi-Fitness Expert Dylan Sarell

Lower body Workouts are those that focus on using your muscles from your legs. These muscles include Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings, Calves and more.

If you are new to Fitness Try to stick with the Beginner workouts! Which could lead to serious injury. Some workouts invlove lifting heavy weights so be careful.

If you are experienced Fitness Expert then you can try any of the Intermediate Workouts.

Deadlifts:

Click this for a how-to-video!

Level: Intermediate

Excersise Description: Have A barbell in a resting position on the floor and then grab it with your knees and legs bent. Keeping your back straight, straighten out your knees while pulling the weight up. (Lift with your knees not your back!) Then stand up perfectly straight with back at a 90 degree angle. Keep repeating the process until you feel you have done enough.

Benefits: Deadlifts are workouts for both upper and lower body. Research also shows that it can reduce lower back pain in the future. It improves jump performance, strengthening your legs. It can also increase your metabolism, losing more calories for weight loss.

Weighted-Step-Ups:

Click this for a how-to-video!

Level: Beginner/Intermediate

Excersise Description: Stand next to an Elevated Platform grab hold of some dumbbells and hold them about shoulder height. Next step on and off the elevated platform. Have one foot on the platform and continually lift up the other foot on and off the platform until you’re satisfied. You can also alternate between lifting up your right and left feet.

Benefits: Weighted Step-ups mimic what is done on a more normal day, like walking up stairs so could heavily improve those day-to-day activities; it strengthens your legs for walking. It is a relatively simple exercise to do and you really don’t need to go to a gym for it.

Squats:

Click this for a how-to-video!

Level: Beginner

Excersise Description: Continually bending and unbending your knees. Stand up straight then bend your knees to a ninety degree angle like you are sitting on an imaginary chair. Keep your back fairly straight and stand straight again and repeat until satisfied.

Benefits: Can help strengthen your legs, improve your everyday walking speed. It can help you lose weight and improve your knees and make them more stable. (more balance.) It can make you more flexible and have stronger bones. Easy to do at home.

Elliptical:

Click this for a how-to-video!

Level: Beginner

Excersise Description: An exercise machine where you run in place using pedals to do strides. You can change the incline to be more steep or level; you can also change the intensity of the pedals. There are two types of elliptical machines. One with Arm levers and one without. The one with arm levers exercises both your lower and upper body. You can do this for a set amount of time until satisfied.

Benefits: Increases your stamina for longer walking or running distances. Can also improve your balance and can be used to lose weight, and burn a lot of calories quickly. It is also a low impact exercise, which doesn’t put too much strain on your muscles/joints.

Lateral Lunge (Side Lunge):

Click this for a how-to-video!

Level: Intermediate

Excersise Description: Stand up straight with two dumbbells in each hand at arm's length. Keeping your right leg straight, step over to the left with your left leg and have each dumbbell on each side of your leg. Pause for a moment then straighten back out and repeat the process with the other leg.

Benefits: Work inner and outer thigh muscles. Help develop more strength in balancing.

Forward Lunge:

Click this for a how-to-video!

Level: Beginner/Intermediate

Excersise Description: Stand up straight with two dumbbells in each hand. Take one leg and move it forward keeping the other leg in place and bending both knees to about 90 degrees. Don’t touch the ground with the rear knee though. Push back to the starting position and repeat the process with the other leg. If you're a beginner Try without dumbbells first.

Benefits: Activates most of your lower body muscles (quads). Increase your core strength and helps with stability. Can be performed at home easily.

Clamshell:

Click this for a how-to-video!

Level: Beginner

Excersise Description: Lay on your side with a ribbon/band tied around both your upper legs. Have one arm hold your head up and the other arm to balance you. Then keeping your feet together, lift up your thigh to as high as it will go then back down again and repeat.

Benefits: Targets your gluteus medius which is responsible for stabilizing your pelvis. Can help your balance in the future which can prevent injury.

Reverse Lunge:

Click this for a how-to-video!

Level: Beginner/Intermediate

Excersise Description: Similar to the forward lunge but go backwards. Standing straight, reach your leg back and crouch down to a 90 degree angle for your front leg. Then straighten out again and repeat with the same leg or switch legs. You can do it with or without dumbbells.

Benefits: Works about every muscle in your lower body like (quads), and can enhance stability. It is also low stress exercise so it can be done by people who may have problems with their legs. Can be done at home.
