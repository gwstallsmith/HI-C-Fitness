Upper body weightlifting involves using weighted equipment like dumbbells and barbells.

Those who use weighted equipment typically want to see their muscle mass and short burst power increase.

Below are weightlifting exercises ranging from beginner, intermediate, and expert experience.

Barbell Bench Press

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Chest, Arms, Shoulders

Description: Grip barbell with hands slightly further than shoulder width apart. Push up until arms are straight. Pull weight down to your chest. Push back up.

Click me for a video example!

Dumbbell Curls

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Arms, Biceps, Forearms

Description: Grip dumbbells underhanded. Start with the weight at your hips. Keep shoulders and elbows in place. Pull the weight up. Once at the top, control the weight back down to your hips.

Click me for a video example!

Dumbbell Overhead Press

Level: Intermediate

Muscle groups: Shoulders, Arms

Description: Grip dumbbells overhand, above your shoulders. Push up until arms are straight. Once at the top, control the weight back down to your shoulders.

Lat Pulldown

Level: Beginner

Muscle groups: Back, Lats

Description: Sit in seat, put legs under cusion. Reach up, and grip bar about shoulder width apart. Pull down to your chest. Control the weight back up.
